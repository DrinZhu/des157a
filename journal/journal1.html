Best Practices For Form Design

2024.01.24

The key takeaway is the emphasis on simplicity, clarity, and user engagement throughout the form-filling process. For example, forms in general have the ability to gather information, but if people find it too much of a hassle and don't do it is the worst possible outcome. So it is very important to design the content of the form in a way that is simple to complete. The specific methods mentioned in the article are: Minimize input fields, that is, reduce unnecessary questions for relevant information; One column layout; ask easy questions first; Minimize dropdown menus and so on.


Another equally important point is to add as much detail as possible to the streamlined form layout. Ambiguous instructions will only make people give up on filling out the form. As mentioned in the article, we should clearly mark required fields; explain input or formatting requirements; provide matching keyboard; provide an option to preview passwords for accuracy; place labels above input fields for better user experience, and so on.


As far as I can remember, I think the best form site I've experienced is the official Apple website. Apple tends to keep forms simple, with clear and concise fields, aligning with the principle of minimizing cognitive load and ensuring a straightforward user experience. It also likely incorporates user-friendly input validation, providing clear feedback on errors or required fields inline, helping people easily correct mistakes and complete the form accurately.
